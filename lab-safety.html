Laboratory Safety & Handling

Guidelines to ensure compound stability and laboratory safety.

Storage Recommendations

To preserve the structural integrity and stability of lyophilized peptide powders, strict storage conditions must be observed:

  • Lyophilized (Dry Powder): Store at -20°C (freezer) in a dark, dry environment. They can remain stable for up to 24 months under these conditions.
  • Reconstituted (Liquid Solution): Once reconstituted with bacteriostatic water, the vial must be stored refrigerated between 2°C and 8°C. Do not freeze reconstituted peptides.
  • Light Exposure: Protect vials from direct UV or sunlight to prevent amino acid degradation.

Safe Laboratory Handling

As with all laboratory chemicals, standard safety protocols are required during handling and analysis:

  • Wash hands thoroughly before and after handling vials.
  • Wipe down your laboratory workspace with 70% isopropyl alcohol before opening vials to prevent environmental contamination.
  • Use sterile, disposable labware whenever transferring solutions.

Protective Equipment (PPE)

When reconstituting or handling raw powders, researchers should utilise standard laboratory protective equipment:

  • Nitrile or latex laboratory gloves to prevent dermal contamination.
  • Safety goggles to protect eyes from accidental splashes during reconstitution.
  • A clean laboratory coat or appropriate covering.

Disposal Protocols

Dispose of empty vials, used labware, and expired compounds in accordance with your institution's biohazard and chemical disposal guidelines. Do not dispose of reconstituted solutions down regular plumbing.
